Matrix layer, which using a less expensive material, is the most important problem to be solved for the wide implementation of MCFCs. It was found, that ceria–based composite is a good oxygen ion conductor used in SOFC applications [2]. It can be expected, that the application of ceria–based porous layers as the MCFC matrix would improve its performance in a less expensive way. The vast majority of electrochemical ceria–based electrolyte support testing was conducted using a very thin matrix layer. This does not correctly reflect the conditions prevailing in the MCFC, where carbonate ions play a decisive role in the transport of reactants and charge carriers. In the present project where a ceria–based porous layer is used as a molten carbonate electrolyte support, this will be tested in a fuel cell on a systematic basis. Existing data is mainly for the short term oxygen ion conductivity of a ceria–based support. In the current project we will conduct systematic tests of dual ion conductivity and long–term stability in MCFC conditions.
Another solution for reduction of LiAlO2 fraction in the matrix is the application of ceramic fibers. It was found that using alumina short fibers reduces the growth of LiAlO2 grains and eliminates matrix cracking [1, 14]. In the proposed project the extension of this idea to other types and forms of fibers will be explored.
The objectives presented in subsection 1.4 aim to improve the performance and durability of the MCFC, which may contribute to the increased implementation of the fuel cell in practical applications.
